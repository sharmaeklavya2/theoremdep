Direction of a convex set

Dependencies:

  1. Convex set
  2. Cone

$d$ is a direction of a convex set $S$ iff $\forall x \in S$, $\{x + \lambda d: \lambda \ge 0\} \subseteq S$.

Let $D$ be the set of directions of $S$. Then $D$ is a cone. $D$ is called the recession cone of $S$.

Proof that $D$ is a cone

Let $d \in D$ and $\alpha \ge 0$. Then $\forall x \in S$ and $\forall \lambda \ge 0$, $x + \lambda (\alpha d) = x + (\lambda\alpha) d \in S$.

Hence, $\forall \alpha \ge 0$ and $\forall d \in D$, $\alpha d \in D$. Hence, $D$ is a cone.
