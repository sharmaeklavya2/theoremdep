Real matrix with real eigenvalues has real eigenvectors

Dependencies:

  • Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors
  • Conjugation of matrices is homomorphic
  • /complex-numbers/complex-numbers

Let $A$ be a real matrix. Let $A$ have a real eigenvalue $\lambda$ and a corresponding complex eigenvector $u$. Then a real eigenvector of $A$ exists corresponding to the eigenvalue $\lambda$.

Proof

\[ Au = \lambda u \implies \overline{A}\,\overline{u} = \overline{\lambda}\,\overline{u} \implies A\overline{u} = \lambda\overline{u} \implies A(u + \overline{u}) = \lambda(u + \overline{u}) \]

Therefore, $v = u + \overline{u}$ is an eigenvector of $A$ corresponding to $\lambda$. Also, $v = u + \overline{u} = 2\operatorname{Re}(u)$ is real.
