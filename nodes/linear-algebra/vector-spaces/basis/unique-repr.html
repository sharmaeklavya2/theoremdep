Unique linear combination of basis

Dependencies:

  • Basis of a vector space

Let $B = \{u_1, u_2, \ldots, u_n\}$ be a finite basis of vector space $V$. Then every vector in $V$ can be expressed uniquely as a linear combination of vectors in $B$.

Proof

Let $v \in V$. Since $B$ spans $V$, every v can be represented as a linear combination of vectors in $B$.

Suppose there are 2 such linear combinations, \[ v = \sum_{i=1}^n a_iu_i = \sum_{i=1}^n b_iu_i \] \[ \Rightarrow 0 = v - v = \sum_{i=1}^n (a_i-b_i)u_i \] Since $B$ is linearly independent, $a_i - b_i = 0$ for all $i$.

Therefore, $a_i = b_i$ for all $i$, which implies that there is a unique representation of $v$ as a linear combination of $B$.
