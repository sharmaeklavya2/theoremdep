Let $R$ be a ring.

$R$ is commutative iff $R[x]$ is commutative.

Proof

Since $R \subset R[x]$, $R[x]$ is commutative implies $R$ is commutative.

Let $R$ be commutative. Let $a(x), b(x) \in R[x]$.

\begin{align} & (ab)_i \\ &= \sum_{j=0}^i a_jb_{i-j} \\ &= \sum_{j=0}^i b_{i-j}a_j \tag{$\because$ $R$ is commutative} \\ &= \sum_{k=0}^i b_ka_{i-k} \tag{$k = i - j$} \\ &= (ba)_i \end{align}

Therefore, $ab = ba$. Therefore, $R[x]$ is commutative.
