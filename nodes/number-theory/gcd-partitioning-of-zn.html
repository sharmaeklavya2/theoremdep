GCD partitioning of Zn

Dependencies:

  1. gcd(a1/d, a2/d, ..., an/d) = gcd(a1, a_2, ..., an)/d

Let $\mathbb{Z}_n = \{0, 1, \ldots, n-1\}$. Let $S_{n,d} = \{x \in \mathbb{Z}_n: \gcd(x, n) = d \}$. Let $\mathbb{Z}_n^* = S_{n,1}$.

Then $\mathbb{Z}_n$ can be partitioned into the sets $\{S_{n,d}: d \mid n\}$, i.e. $S_{n,x} \cap S_{n,y} = \{\}$ for all $x \neq y$ and $\mathbb{Z}_n = \bigcup_{d \mid n} S_{n,d}$.

Also, \[ S_{n,d} = \begin{cases}d \mathbb{Z}_{\frac{n}{d}}^* & \textrm{if } d \mid n \\ \{\} & \textrm{otherwise} \end{cases} \]

Proof

It is trivial to see that the sets $S_{n,1}, S_{n,2}, \ldots$ partition $\mathbb{Z}_n$ completely and $S_{n,d}$ is non-empty iff $d \mid n$.

The only thing left to prove is $S_{n,d} = d \mathbb{Z}_{\frac{n}{d}}^*$ when $d \mid n$.

\begin{align} & x \in S_{n,d} \\ &\iff \gcd(x, n) = d \wedge 0 \le x < n \\ &\iff \gcd\left(\frac{x}{d}, \frac{n}{d}\right) = 1 \wedge 0 \le \frac{x}{d} < \frac{n}{d} \\ &\iff \frac{x}{d} \in \mathbb{Z}_{\frac{n}{d}}^* \\ &\iff x \in d \mathbb{Z}_{\frac{n}{d}}^* \end{align}

Therefore, $S_{n,d} = d \mathbb{Z}_{\frac{n}{d}}^*$.
