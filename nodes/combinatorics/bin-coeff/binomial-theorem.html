Binomial theorem

Dependencies:

  1. Semiring
  2. Binomial Coefficient

Let $a$ and $b$ be elements of a commutative semiring $R$. For a non-negative integer $n$ \[ (a+b)^n = \sum_{i=0}^n \binom{n}{i} a^{n-i} b^i \]

Proof

Expanding the product of the $n$ linear forms, we get $2^n$ product forms. Product forms of the type $a^{n-i}b^i$ are those which took $b$ from $i$ of the linear terms and $a$ from the rest of the linear terms. Since there are $\binom{n}{i}$ ways of choosing $i$ linear forms out of $n$, there are $\binom{n}{i}$ product terms of the form $a^{n-i}b^i$. Therefore, the coefficient of $a^{n-i}b^i$ is $\binom{n}{i}$.

An alternative proof is possible using mathematical induction over $n$ and the additive-recursive form of the binomial coefficient.
