Measurable function

Dependencies:

  1. σ-algebra

Let $(X, E)$ and $(Y, F)$ be $\sigma$-algebras. Let $f: X \mapsto Y$ be a function.

Let $T \subseteq Y$. Define $f^{-1}(T) = \{x \in X: f(x) \in T\}$.

$f$ is said to be measurable iff $\forall T \in F, f^{-1}(T) \in E$.
