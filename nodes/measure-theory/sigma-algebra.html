σ-algebra

Dependencies:

  1. /sets-and-relations/countable-set

Let $X$ be a set and let $F$ be a subset of the power-set of $X$. We say that $(X, F)$ is a $\sigma$-algebra (or $F$ is a $sigma$-algebra over $X$) iff all of these properties hold:

  1. $X \in F$.
  2. closure under complementation: $A \in F \implies X-A \in F$. $X-A$ is called the complement of $A$, and is denoted by $\overline{A}$.
  3. closure under countable unions: Let $S = \{A_1, A_2, \ldots\} \in F$ be a countable set. Then $\left(\bigcup_{A \in S} A\right) \in F$.

As a trivial example, $\{\{\}, X\}$ is a $\sigma$-algebra over $X$ and the power-set of $X$ is a $\sigma$-algebra over $X$.
