Degree of sum of polynomials

Dependencies:

  • Polynomial

Let $p, q \in R[x]$. Then $\operatorname{deg}(p+q) \le \operatorname{deg}(p) + \operatorname{deg}(q)$.

Proof

Let $k = \operatorname{max}(\operatorname{deg}(p), \operatorname{deg}(q))$. Then $\forall i > k, (p+q)_i = p_i + q_i = 0 + 0 = 0$.

Therefore, $p+q \in R[x]$ and $\operatorname{deg}(p+q) \le k$.
